About Me

I'm a data scientist who's passionate about applying machine learning and data-driven methods to enhance safety and well-being. I hold a BS in Computer Science from Texas A&M University and an MS in Data Science and Machine Learning from Northwestern University. After starting my career in New York City, I am now based in Austin, TX.


For the past eight years, I’ve worked at the University of Chicago Crime Lab, where I build and evaluate machine learning models aimed at reducing gun violence and advancing justice. My projects often involve designing and testing prediction models that are accurate, fair, and tailored to their real-world use case. I also work on impact evaluations of violence-reduction interventions, using both randomized control trials and quasi-experimental methods.


Additionally, I maintain Name Match, an open-source probabilistic record linkage library that combines machine learning with user-defined constraints to accurately and efficiently link records that refer to the same individual.


My expertise includes:

  • Translating real-world problems into research questions and testable hypotheses
  • Designing clean, reproducible, and scalable data pipelines
  • Predictive modeling, causal inference, and record linkage
  • Recognizing and debugging suspicious code and outputs
  • Mentorship, team leadership, and managing deliverables

  • Programming in python, R, and SQL
